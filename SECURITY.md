# Security policy

This document outlines the security protocols and vulnerability reporting guidelines for the ScaleX project. Ensuring the security of our systems is a top priority.

## Reporting a vulnerability

If you discover a security vulnerability, please report it responsibly via a private channel rather than opening a public GitHub issue. Include any information needed to reproduce and assess the issue.

To ensure a responsible and effective disclosure process, please adhere to the following:

- Maintain confidentiality and refrain from publicly disclosing the vulnerability until we have had the opportunity to investigate and address the issue.
- Refrain from running automated vulnerability scans on our infrastructure without prior consent.
- Do not exploit any discovered vulnerabilities for malicious purposes.
- Do not engage in physical security attacks, social engineering, DDoS attacks, spam campaigns, or attacks on third-party applications as part of your testing.

## Out of scope

The following types of vulnerabilities are considered out of scope:

- Vulnerabilities requiring man-in-the-middle (MITM) attacks or physical access to a user's device.
- Content spoofing or text injection issues without a clear attack vector.
- Issues related to email spoofing.
- Missing DNSSEC, CAA, or CSP headers.
- Absence of secure or HTTP-only flags on non-sensitive cookies.

## Our commitment

- **Response time** — Acknowledgement of your report and an estimated timeline for resolution.
- **Confidentiality** — Your report will be treated with confidentiality.
- **Recognition** — With your permission, we will publicly acknowledge your contribution once resolved.

Thank you for working with us to keep ScaleX safe.
